"What's the led screen price?" is the first question every buyer asks, and the hardest one to answer with a single number. Two quotes for what looks like the same wall can land lakhs apart, and the buyer staring at both has no way to tell which one is fair. The gap rarely comes from padded margins. It comes from five or six real cost drivers stacked on top of each other - pitch, environment, cabinet grade, refresh rate. Once you know what they are, an led display price stops feeling like a guess and starts reading like a spec sheet.

Fig. 1 - Prices of different Pixel Pitch

Pixel Pitch Is the Biggest Lever on LED Display Price

Pixel pitch - the distance between LEDs - decides how many diodes sit on every panel. A tighter pitch packs in more diodes, more driver electronics, and more assembly labour per module, so it costs more per square foot. This single spec is why an indoor led display price for a boardroom looks nothing like a stadium screen quote, even though both are technically "LED screens."

Indoor vs Outdoor LED Display: Different Builds, Different Price

An outdoor led display needs a sealed IP65+ cabinet, a heavier power supply to push brightness past direct sunlight, and weatherproof connectors an indoor panel never touches. That's why outdoor led screen price per square foot usually sits well above an equivalent indoor unit, even at the same pitch. A buyer comparing a rooftop hoarding quote in Delhi against a showroom indoor led display in Jaipur is, structurally, pricing two different products - not the same screen with a discount attached.

Cabinet Material and Refresh Rate Move the Number Too

Die-cast aluminium cabinets cost more than steel or iron but hold tolerance after repeated handling, which matters for anything that gets moved. A higher refresh rate - needed for broadcast or camera-facing screens - adds cost as well, since it demands faster driver ICs on every module. Neither shows up as its own line item, but both sit quietly inside every serious led display price or led screen price quote.

Rental-Grade LED Panels Are a Different Product, Not a Different Deal

Led screen rental panels aren't a pricing plan - they're a build. Event and production companies across Rajasthan and Delhi who set up stage backdrops, wedding walls, and trade show displays need a panel light enough to load into a truck, quick-lock cabinets that rig in minutes, and a frame that survives being set up and struck dozens of times a year. That's a lighter cabinet, tighter module tolerances, and road-case packaging, priced as its own category, separate from a fixed indoor or outdoor led video wall at the same pitch.

These rental-grade panels are sold outright to the event and rental businesses that put screens on the ground for weddings, launches, and concerts. They own the fleet and rent it out to their own clients - the panel purchase itself is a one-time cost, priced by pitch and cabinet spec like any other led display, just engineered to survive constant transport.

Costs Buyers Forget to Ask About

  • Structural mounting and rigging, which can add 10–20% on top of panel cost
  • Control system and processor, often sold separately from the panel itself
  • Spare modules and driver boards, kept aside for after-sales repair
  • Content management software, if the screen runs as digital signage rather than static content

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an LED screen cost in India?
Indoor fine-pitch panels typically start around ₹15,000 per square foot; outdoor-rated led display panels start higher, often ₹35,000 and up, before mounting, control system, and installation.

Why is outdoor LED display price higher than indoor?
Outdoor units carry a sealed IP65+ cabinet, stronger power supply, and higher brightness output to stay visible in direct sunlight - all of which add cost that an indoor panel never needs.

Is a rental-grade LED panel cheaper than a standard one?
Not necessarily. Rental-grade panels use lighter cabinets and quick-lock hardware built for constant transport, which is a different spec, not automatically a lower price.

Does pixel pitch affect price the most?
Yes. Pixel pitch decides diode density, which drives most of the cost difference between an led display board for a boardroom and one for a highway billboard.

An led screen price that looks too low almost always means a spec got dropped somewhere, not that the buyer found a deal. Match pitch, environment, and cabinet grade across every quote before comparing numbers, and the real cost of your led display stops being a mystery and starts being a spec sheet you control.
